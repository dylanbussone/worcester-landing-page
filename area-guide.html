Essentials

Big Y
A full-service grocery store just a 2-minute drive from the house.

Price Chopper
Another great grocery option, about 4 minutes away, known for its excellent bakery.

7-Eleven
Located right across the street, perfect for grabbing everyday essentials quickly and conveniently.

Dining and Night Life

Joey’s Bar & Grill
A welcoming sports bar great for all ages. There’s plenty of parking, but it’s also just a 10-minute walk from the house.

Scruffy Murphy’s
One of my favorite haunts when I’m in town. They offer nightly specials that are hard to beat, and the vibe is always high-energy and fun.

Sushi Miyazawa
In my opinion, the best sushi in Worcester—and only a 6-minute walk away! It’s a cozy BYOB spot with just about 10 seats, so expect a wait if dining in. Luckily, takeout is quick and reliable.

The Sole Proprietor
Worcester’s go-to for fresh seafood. Insider tip: ask for the lunch menu at dinner—you’ll usually get the same great options (like a ¼ lb lobster roll) for a bit less.

Coney Island
A Worcester legend. Order it with chili for the full experience. There’s street parking, but also a lot beside the building if you don’t find a spot.

The Mercantile
A newer addition to the Worcester dining scene that made an instant impression. Great food, amazing service—you really can’t go wrong here.

Peppercorn’s Grill
A nostalgic classic for me—I’ve been coming here since I was a kid. A go-to dinner spot where you’re likely to bump into someone you know.

Dalat
Serving pho and boba long before it was trendy—my parents even went on dates here 36 years ago. If you’re into avocado, don’t miss the avocado smoothie.

Worcester Public Market
A lively food court with local vendors—perfect for when no one can agree on what to eat. Great energy and options for everyone.

The Boynton
Another Worcester institution. Grab a beer, catch up with friends, and enjoy the neighborhood vibe. Plenty of parking in the back. Fun fact: my dad proposed to my mom at the bar.

Jason’s Pizza
Your go-to neighborhood pizzeria, just around the corner. I’ve been going here my whole life—my grandparents lived across the street and would give me $5 for a small cheese pizza and expect change. It might cost a bit more now, but it’s still a gem.

Mezcal
Classy, modern Mexican cuisine—and the only place I’ll get guac in Worcester.

Thai Corner
Right around the corner from the house. My husband is a Thai food fan and gives it a big thumbs up.
